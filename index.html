My "-Saurus" Websites

The "(dino)saurus" websites are a collection of some of my more refined frontend development projects I have created to familiarize myself with HTML/CSS/JS. The deployment of my first "-saurus" website, Weathosaurus, was related to my participation in Science Olympiad's meteorology event. All these names were inspired by Aurorasaurus.org, an aurora borealis sightings tracker.


  • Weathosaurus:
    Website fetches real-time data for your location search from OpenWeatherAPI. Creates a simple station model with JS as well.

  • Geolosaurus:
    A one-page geology glossary with many categories.

  • Quizaurus:
    A Quizlet-like flashcard website that saves user-made flashcards locally. Enter a word and its definition, and you can study with the flashcard-like feature. Website contains a simplified version of Quizlet's Write feature. I created this to study French vocabulary, since Quizlet's features are no longer free.

  • Typosaurus:
    A 60 seconds typing game (made with a tutorial). Calculates your words per minute by having you type randomly generated words.
